Pipeline intent: each stage reduces bias from class imbalance and keeps the final model usable for real-world fraud operations.

Workflow Stages

Each stage is designed to reduce bias introduced by the heavy class imbalance and to make the final model decision-ready.

1. ExploreInspect distributions, correlations, and outliers.
2. BalanceApply undersampling, oversampling, or SMOTE.
3. TrainFit the Random Forest model on sampled data.
4. CompareCheck precision, recall, and ROC-AUC.
5. TuneSelect the best business threshold.
6. DeployUse the risk score for alerting and review.

Sampling Logic

Undersampling reduces the number of majority-class examples so the classifier cannot ignore the fraud class. Oversampling duplicates or expands the minority class. SMOTE creates synthetic minority points so the boundary becomes more informative.

Model Choice

Random Forest is a strong and stable baseline for the current build. It works well for fraud detection because it captures complex relationships without requiring manual feature equations.
